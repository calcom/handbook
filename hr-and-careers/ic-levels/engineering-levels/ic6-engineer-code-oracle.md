# 🙇 IC6 Engineer (Code Oracle)

* **Role**: IC6s (Principal Engineers) are the most senior individual contributors at Cal.com. They operate at the org and company level: setting technical strategy, defining the shape of the platform for the next several years, and directly partnering with the Head of Engineering to decide the biggest architectural bets. A Principal Engineer's decisions shape the long-term technical trajectory of Cal.com: what we build, what we rebuild, what we retire, and what the codebase and stack look like two years from now.
* **Responsibilities**:
  * Be the **primary technical partner to the Head of Engineering** on company-wide architecture, platform direction, and large-scale refactors. Together you decide what gets rebuilt, what gets consolidated, and what gets sunset, and you often lead the execution of those bets personally.
  * Own the long-horizon technical roadmap. You're the one thinking about where Cal.com's architecture needs to be in 18-24 months, and you're writing the docs and making the calls that get us there.
  * **Set the company's AI engineering strategy.** Decide which AI tools we standardize on, what our internal developer experience with AI looks like, how we evaluate AI-generated contributions, and what our posture is on autonomous agents writing production code. You're the person the rest of the company looks to for a credible answer to "how should we be using AI?"
  * **Define Cal.com's AI-native product surface.** Partner with the Head of Engineering, Head of Product, and founders on the technical design for agent-assisted scheduling, AI-driven availability, and any product-facing AI feature. You personally own the hardest parts of the architecture.
  * Own the most consequential technical decisions at the company: database architecture, multi-region strategy, platform boundaries, monorepo structure, framework adoption, and major rewrites. You drive these with rigor (written proposals, prototypes, and data), not opinions.
  * Pull in and mentor IC4s and IC5s so they can grow into the next generation of Principal Engineers and technical leaders. Succession is part of the role.
  * Be a strong voice in leveling, hiring, and performance calibration for the entire engineering team.
  * When a critical incident threatens the business, you're one of the handful of people who can be counted on to own it end-to-end, across any system.
* **Leadership**:
  * IC6s lead through influence and clarity of thought. You are often the deciding vote in the room, not because of hierarchy, but because you've earned the team's trust by being right more often, for better reasons, than anyone else.
  * You hold the whole engineering team to a high bar while also being the person who makes the bar reachable, through better docs, better tools, better patterns, and better mentorship.
  * You are calm under the hardest outages and thoughtful under the hardest trade-offs. When a decision is genuinely difficult, the team wants you in the room.
  * You protect the team's focus. You help the Head of Engineering say no to the wrong work and yes to the right work, and you explain the reasoning in a way that the team can internalize.
* **Expertise**:
  * You are a true generalist at senior depth: deeply expert in multiple areas of Cal.com (e.g., scheduling, calendar sync, infrastructure, billing, embeds) and conversant everywhere else.
  * You understand our stack at the level of "why we chose this and what we'd do differently": TypeScript, Prisma, Next.js, our deployments, our monorepo, our data model, our testing strategy, our eval pipelines for AI.
  * You can pick up unfamiliar code, ours or open-source, and reason about its behavior, correctness, and performance quickly. You don't wait for someone to explain a system to you; you read it.
  * You have strong, well-calibrated opinions about where the broader software industry is going (AI tooling, agentic workflows, runtime platforms, developer experience) and you translate those opinions into good calls for Cal.com.
  * Your code is, as at IC4, effectively flawless, but the more important output at this level is the code and the systems _others_ write because of decisions you made.
  * You write the kind of design docs and post-mortems that the rest of the team learns from.
* **How IC6 differs from IC5**: As a small team, both of you have impact that is felt across the whole team, so the distinction is not about reach. It's about horizon, posture, and the kind of decision being made.
  * IC5 runs the practice; you set the strategy. An IC5 writes the prompt libraries the team uses. You decide whether we're a Claude Code shop, a Cursor shop, or both, and why.
  * An IC5 shapes what we're shipping this quarter and next. You shape where the codebase, the stack, and the engineering practice are heading 18-24 months out.
  * An IC5 is a strong voice in architecture debates. You're often the deciding vote in those rooms because the team has learned to trust your judgment.
  * IC5 is the most senior level where an engineer is still measured primarily by engineering outcomes. IC6 is measured by the long-term technical trajectory of the company.

**A note on the ceiling**: IC6 is the top of Cal.com's individual contributor ladder as currently defined. Growth at IC6 is lateral and deep (broader influence, more consequential bets, sharper technical judgment) rather than another level up. If you're operating at IC6 and looking for more, the conversation is about scope, ownership, and impact with the Head of Engineering, not about a next band.
